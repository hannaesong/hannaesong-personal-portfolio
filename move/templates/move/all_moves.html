Timeline

Hanna has been to 19 Countries

Here I seek to give a simple timeline of the major moves I've had during the course of my life.


--May 2000-March 2003--

Dusseldorf, Germany

The land of my birth: Germany. My parents, freshly married, moved to Germany after their wedding, and I came soon after.

My younger sister was born a year and a half later.

While studying in Germany, my father was given the oppurtunity to join an NGO and go to Afghanistan. The rest is history.

--July 2003-January 2004--

Leeds, England

Before going into Afghanistan, our family went to live in England for six months.

This is where I started picking up English, while forgetting my German.

I guess this is where my first language was decided.

--April 2004-May 2007--

Kabul, Afghanistan

This is where I first went to school. I remember being so excited to go to kindergarten before my fifth birthday.

I had amazing teachers and great friends. As a kindergartener, I remember looking up at the sixth graders and wondering when I would be like them.

Quite a few interesting stories from this time. I don't remember everything, but I look back on Afghanistan with a tender heart.

--July 2007-October 2008--

Daejeon, Korea

After the incident in July 2007 with the Koreans and the Taliban, our family could no longer stay in Afghanistan. So, for a while, we lived in Korea.

This is the first and last time I ever attended a Korean public school for first and second grade.

My youngest sister was born at this time. She was a cute surprise baby.

--November 2008-July 2011--

Peshawar, Pakistan

These are perhaps the happiest years of my life so far.

Many people left as we came in, leaving only a handful of foreigners in the country. At its lowest point, there were only two families and two ladies in our city. School was a blast.

But eventually our family had to leave as well. And since it looked like we wouldn't be settling down anytime soonâ€“-an accurate forecast--my sister and I started homeschooling.

--April 2012-July 2012--

Istanbul, Turkey

Three months living at Fatih, Istanbul and a few days at a resort by the sea.

Sounds nice, no? Hikes by the beach, walks through the Grand Bazaar, fresh fish.

But also the time I started homeschooling in earnest. Not a walk in the park. Took a while to get used to.

--August 2012-August 2013--

Dushanbe, Tajikistan

Our family stayed in Tajikistan for one year.

Although there was an international school, we decided to continue homeschooling. Despite that, I was able to make friend there, a few of whom were homeschooled as well.

Started learning Russian a few months before we left. I am only now able to introduce myself, but I do remember that Russian numbers are quite complicated.

--October 2013-December 2017--

Kathmandu, Nepal

I lived in Nepal on an off for about four years. Never thought I'd be in Nepal, let alone live there, but life's full of surprises.

We were in Kathmandu during the earthquake of April, 2015. It was a normal Saturday morning; my sisters and I were watching animal videos when the first earthquake hit.

Overall, a very nice place to live. The sunsets are by far the most beautiful here. And if you ever visit, I would definitely recommend getting a cup of coffee.

--April 2016-February 2018--

Daejeon, Korea

Life back in Korea was a strange change of pace. This was the first time I felt at home in my 'home' country. Ironic, I know.

I ordered my first delievery pizza when I was sixteen. You have no idea how accomplished I felt. It was truly a life-changing event.

--March 2018-Present--

Duisburg, Germany

Finally after fifteen years, I have returned to the country of my birth, a place I hadn't dreamed of being in again.

I completed my high school education attending a couple German language courses. The plan was then to enroll into a university here. That was the plan.

Long story short, I found myself in an interesting crack in the system. Uncertainty, my ever-present, ever-faithful companion, yet accompanies me.

I suppose I have much to learn from her still.
